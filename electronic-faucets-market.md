<p><ul><li>Touchless Electronic Faucets</li><li>Touched Electronic Faucets</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The electronic faucets market comprises primarily two types: touchless and touched electronic faucets. Touchless electronic faucets utilize infrared sensors to detect motion, allowing users to operate them without physical contact, promoting hygiene and convenience. In contrast, touched electronic faucets require users to physically engage a sensor or button to activate water flow. Both types cater to diverse preferences and settings, with touchless models often favored in public restrooms for their efficiency, while touched models are popular in residential kitchens and bathrooms.</p></p>
