<p><p>The immunohistochemistry (IHC) testing market encompasses various techniques used for detecting specific antigens in tissue samples. The traditional direct method employs labeled primary antibodies to bind directly to antigens. The new direct method enhances sensitivity and specificity with advanced labeling techniques. The enzyme bridge method utilizes enzymatic reactions for signal amplification, while the PAP (Peroxidase-Antiperoxide) method involves a secondary antibody linked to an enzyme, improving detection limits. These methods collectively support diagnostic advancements in pathology and oncology.</p></p>
