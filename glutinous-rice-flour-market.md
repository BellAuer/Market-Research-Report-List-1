<p><p>The glutinous rice flour market is segmented into two main types: dried glutinous rice flour and water-milled glutinous rice flour. Dried glutinous rice flour is produced by drying and grinding glutinous rice, resulting in a fine powder used in various culinary applications, especially in Asian cuisine. Water-milled glutinous rice flour, on the other hand, is made using a traditional method that involves soaking washed rice and grinding it with water, yielding a moister texture, often favored for specific recipes and dishes.</p></p>
