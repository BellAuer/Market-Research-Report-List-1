<p><p>The plant-based nanocellulose market is primarily divided into two types: Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC) and Nanocrystalline Cellulose (NCC). NFC is characterized by its network of long, flexible nanofibers, which provide superior mechanical properties and are widely used in food, cosmetics, and packaging applications. In contrast, NCC consists of rigid, crystalline nanoparticles with high strength and thermal stability, making it suitable for advanced materials, pharmaceuticals, and composites. Both types demonstrate unique properties that cater to various industrial needs.</p></p>
