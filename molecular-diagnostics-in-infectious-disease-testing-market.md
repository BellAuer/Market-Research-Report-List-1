<p><strong>The Molecular Diagnostics in Infectious Disease Testing Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)</li><li>Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification Technology (INAAT)</li><li>Microarrays</li><li>Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)</li><li>Hybridization</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Molecular diagnostics for infectious disease testing encompasses various techniques. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) amplifies DNA for detection of pathogens. Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification Technology (INAAT) allows for amplification at constant temperatures, enhancing speed and simplicity. Microarrays enable simultaneous analysis of multiple targets on a single chip. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) provides comprehensive genomic data to identify pathogens precisely. Hybridization methods involve the binding of nucleic acid probes to target sequences, while "Others" include emerging techniques like CRISPR-based diagnostics and technologies not falling into previous categories.</p></p>
