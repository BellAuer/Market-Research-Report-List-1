<p><p>Chlorinated Paraffin Wax is categorized into three market types based on its chlorine content and application: L-grade, M-grade, and H-grade. L-grade, with low chlorine content, is used in applications requiring flexibility, such as coatings and plastics. M-grade has moderate chlorine levels, making it suitable for use in metalworking fluids and lubricants. H-grade, with high chlorine content, is primarily employed in fire-resistant materials and certain industrial applications, offering enhanced stability and chemical resistance for demanding environments.</p></p>
