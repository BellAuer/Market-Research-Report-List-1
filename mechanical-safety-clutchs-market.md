<p><p>The mechanical safety clutch market comprises three primary types: chimeric, friction, and fracture. Chimeric clutches blend elements from various designs to optimize performance and adaptability. Friction clutches use friction between surfaces to transmit torque while allowing for slip under overload conditions, providing protection for machinery. Fracture clutches operate based on the mechanical failure of components under extreme stress, safeguarding the system from damage. Each type serves distinct applications, enhancing safety and reliability in mechanical systems.</p></p>
