<p><p>Bioelectronics and biosensors encompass various technologies designed to detect biological signals and analyze substances. Electrochemical biosensors utilize electrochemical reactions for sensing, making them ideal for glucose monitoring and disease detection. Thermal biosensors track temperature variations linked to biochemical reactions, suitable for monitoring metabolic activities. Piezoelectric biosensors detect mass changes through frequency shifts, useful in biomolecule detection. Optical biosensors employ light signals to measure concentrations, offering high sensitivity for various applications, including environmental monitoring and medical diagnostics.</p></p>
