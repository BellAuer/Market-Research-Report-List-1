<p><p>Neopentyl Glycol (NPG) is an important intermediate in producing resins, plasticizers, and coatings. The market is segmented into two main production methods: the disproportionation method and the hydrogenation method. The disproportionation method involves the cleavage of isobutylene, leading to various products, while the hydrogenation method utilizes catalytic reduction of formaldehyde and isobutylene. Each method influences the purity, cost, and environmental impact of NPG, thereby affecting market demand and applications across industries such as automotive, construction, and consumer goods.</p></p>
