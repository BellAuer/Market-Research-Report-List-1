<p><p>The underwater switches market primarily features limit switches and proximity switches, both designed for harsh aquatic environments. Limit switches are used to detect the presence or position of underwater objects or barriers, providing precise control in applications like robotics and marine automation. Proximity switches, on the other hand, detect nearby objects without direct contact, utilizing technologies such as capacitive or inductive sensors. These switches enhance safety and efficiency in various underwater operations, including subsea exploration and monitoring systems.</p></p>
