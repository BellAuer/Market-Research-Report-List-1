<p><p>The specular microscopes market is divided into two primary types: contact and non-contact specular microscopes. Contact specular microscopes involve direct contact with the eye or cornea to capture detailed images, providing high-resolution data on corneal structure and health. Non-contact specular microscopes, on the other hand, utilize light to observe the cornea without physical touch, enhancing patient comfort and reducing the risk of infection. Both types are crucial for assessing various ocular conditions and guiding treatment decisions.</p></p>
