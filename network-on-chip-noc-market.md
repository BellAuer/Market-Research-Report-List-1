<p><p>Network-on-Chip (NoC) is an advanced communication architecture designed for integrating multiple cores on a single chip, enhancing data transfer efficiency. The market is segmented into direct and indirect topology types. Direct topology features point-to-point connections among cores, facilitating straightforward communication but limited scalability. In contrast, indirect topology employs switching elements, allowing for more flexible and scalable connections, improving overall performance and reducing congestion. Both types cater to varying needs in complex integrated systems, influencing design choices in semiconductor applications.</p></p>
